Okay enough learning, time to build!

Building projects is important for web development, because it allows you to put your skills and knowledge into practice, and develop real-world solutions for clients or for your own use. It also allows you to experiment with new technologies and features, and to create a portfolio of work that you can show to potential employers or clients. A good project should have:

  • A clear and well-defined goal or purpose
  • A user-centered design, with a focus on usability and accessibility
  • A well-structured and organized codebase, with good commenting and documentation
  • A responsive and mobile-friendly design
  • Adequate testing and debugging to ensure that the project is stable and free of bugs.
